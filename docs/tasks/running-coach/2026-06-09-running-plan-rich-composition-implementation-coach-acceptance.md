## Acceptance Decision

Accepted for create-path architecture planning.

This was not a claim that perfect training richness had been achieved. It meant the matrix was
coach-quality acceptable, family-specific enough, honest enough, safe enough, and differentiated
enough to move from backend matrix refinement into create-path planning.

Historical scope note:

- The unavailable counts in this pass reflected the pre-universal-no-dead-end state.
- Coach-plausible beginner Half and Marathon Base runner-level blocks were superseded by later
  no-dead-end bridge/extension doctrine.

## Family Verdicts

- `10K`: accepted across supported matrix cases. Conservative 10K softened by dropping sharper work
  while keeping turnover and endpoint honesty.
- `Half Marathon`: accepted after prior conservative-load weakness was repaired enough to preserve
  half-specific durability and long-run role identity.
- `Marathon Base`: accepted as a base-building family, not a race-readiness family. Conservative
  cases kept time-on-feet and steady-finish identity without leaking into exact `42195m` completion.

Matrix-level evidence:

- `468` total scenarios.
- `247` preview-ready scenarios.
- `221` unavailable scenarios, driven mostly by honest blockers:
  `unsupported_runner_level_for_family`, `insufficient_available_days`, `long_run_day_blocked`, and
  `builder_validation_failed`.
- Fake pace and fake personal HR remained absent.
- Endpoint honesty remained intact for supported families.

## Product Rules Preserved

- Create-path planning may proceed only if it preserves backend-owned composition grammar as canonical truth.
- Unavailable scenarios must remain honest product states, not silently degraded generic plans.
- Review/create surfaces must expose visible family identity so runners can distinguish:
  10K turnover/repeatability, Half durability, and Marathon Base time-on-feet durability.
- Conservative-load scenarios must preserve family identity through softer signals, not by removing identity entirely.

## What Not To Change

- do not weaken metric-truth boundaries
- do not add fake pace from target time
- do not add fake personal HR
- do not force weekly intervals for visual variety
- do not turn Marathon Base into a hidden marathon race plan
- do not flatten backend richness into generic visible workout naming in future UI/create-path work

## Supersession

This acceptance remains the historical proof that the rich-composition matrix cleared create-path
planning. Later no-dead-end and anti-flatness slices refined the long-horizon bridge/extension
behavior while preserving the safety boundaries above.
